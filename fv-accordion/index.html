Buying a home is one of the most common long-term financial goals. You have money saved today — but will it be enough if you simply invest it and let it grow? TVM gives us a precise answer to that question.

Consider the following scenario:

  • You have $12,000 saved today and invest it in a high-yield account
  • You make no additional contributions — you let it grow on its own
  • The account earns 6.0% APR
  • You plan to buy in 5 years and need $15,000 for a down payment
  • Will you have enough for the $15,000 down payment?

Enter the following values into your HP 10BII+ calculator in any order, then solve for FV.

Note: Clear your calculator before starting a new TVM problem to avoid using old values.

InputKeyWhat it represents
5NTotal periods (5 years)
6I/YRAnnual interest rate (6% APR)
−12000PVLump sum invested today (cash out = negative)
0PMTNo recurring contributions

Press FV to solve.

FV = $16,058.71
PV is entered as negative because the money is leaving your pocket today. The calculator returns FV as a positive number — money coming back to you in the future. Since $16,058.71 > $15,000, yes — you will have enough for the down payment, with $1,058.71 to spare.

Since $16,058.71 exceeds the $15,000 goal, yes — you will have enough, with $1,058.71 to spare.

With no recurring payments, solving for FV is a single clean step — just apply the compound growth formula to the lump sum.

Future value formula
FV = PV × (1 + r)ⁿ
Substitute the known values
FV = 12,000 × (1 + 0.06)⁵
FV = 12,000 × (1.06)⁵
FV = 12,000 × 1.338226
FV = $16,058.71
Answer the question
FV of $16,058.71 > Goal of $15,000.00
Yes — you will have enough, with $1,058.71 to spare.
Where: r = annual rate (6% = 0.06) and n = total periods (5 years). Because there are no payments, you only need one formula — no annuity calculation required.
