Saving for a college fund is one of the most important long-term financial goals. A typical target for a four-year degree can be significant — and TVM gives us a precise answer to how long consistent saving will take to get you there.

Consider the following scenario:

  • A lump sum of $15,000 is deposited into a high-yield savings account today
  • An additional $3,000 is contributed every year
  • The account earns 6.0% APR
  • The goal is to reach $50,000

Enter the following values into your HP 10BII+ calculator in any order, then solve for N.

Note: Clear your calculator before starting a new TVM problem to avoid using old values.

InputKeyWhat it represents
6I/YRAnnual interest rate (6% APR)
−15000PVLump sum deposited today (cash out = negative)
−3000PMTAnnual contribution (cash out = negative)
50000FVSavings goal (cash in = positive)

Press N to solve.

N ≈ 7.39 years
PV and PMT are entered as negative because money is flowing out of your pocket. FV is positive because it is money flowing back to you. The calculator solves for how many periods are needed to bridge the gap.

Use Excel's built-in =NPER() function. Set up your spreadsheet by entering labels in column A and values in column B, then write the formula in cell B5.

Pro tip: Using cell references like B1 instead of typing 6% directly means you can change your savings goal or rate and instantly see how it affects your timeline — no rewriting the formula needed.

When both a lump sum and recurring payments are involved, we use logarithms to isolate and solve for n.

The combined FV equation
FV = PV × (1 + r)ⁿ + PMT × [((1 + r)ⁿ − 1) / r]
Step 1 — Substitute the known values
50,000 = 15,000 × (1.06)ⁿ + 3,000 × [((1.06)ⁿ − 1) / 0.06]
Step 2 — Simplify by letting x = (1.06)ⁿ
50,000 = 15,000x + 50,000x − 50,000
100,000 = 65,000x
x ≈ 1.5385
Step 3 — Solve for n using logarithms
(1.06)ⁿ = 1.5385
n = ln(1.5385) / ln(1.06)
n ≈ 7.39 years
Where: r = annual rate (6% = 0.06). The logarithm step works because ln(aⁿ) = n × ln(a), which lets us bring the exponent down and solve directly.
