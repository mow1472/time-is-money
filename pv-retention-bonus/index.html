Understanding present value helps you make smarter decisions about money you'll receive in the future. A dollar today is worth more than a dollar tomorrow — and TVM lets us calculate exactly how much less that future money is worth right now.

Consider the following scenario:

  • Your employer promises a $10,000 retention bonus
  • You won't receive it for 4 years
  • You could otherwise earn 8.0% APR on your money
  • How much is that future bonus worth in today's dollars?

Enter the following values into your HP 10BII+ calculator in any order, then solve for PV.

Note: Clear your calculator before starting a new TVM problem to avoid using old values.

Press PV to solve.

PV = −$7,350.30
The calculator returns PV as a negative number because it represents money flowing out — what you would need to invest today. The economic equivalent is $7,350.30: having that amount today, invested at 8%, grows to exactly $10,000 in four years.

Excel returns PV as a negative number (shown in parentheses) because it represents a cash outflow — the amount you'd need to set aside today.

When there are no recurring payments, divide the future value by the growth factor.

Present value formula
PV = FV / (1 + r)ⁿ
PV = 10,000 / (1 + 0.08)⁴
PV = 10,000 / 1.36049
PV = $7,350.30
Where: r = annual rate (8% = 0.08) and n = total periods (4 years). The result is positive here because we're solving for what you'd need today — the cash inflow equivalent.
